Introduction

The following is a description of the of the various roles that provide data professional services in Integrant. These roles are divided into 5 chapters: Data Engineering, Data Science, Data Analysis, DataOps Engineering and MLOps Engineering. We will describe the responsbilites for each chapter, then list the various roles that exist within that chapter. For every role listed, we will provide a scorecard that details the skills expected and proficiency level for the role at hand. Proficiency levels are based on a scale from 1 to 5, with 1 being a novice proficiency and 5 begin an expert level proficieny unless otherwise stated.

Data Engineering

Data Engineers are responsible for the acquisition, ingestion, storage, processing, transformation and presentation of data for analytical consumption. These core responsibilities are articulated in the form of an architecture that is composed of a number of pipelines and data stores that handle data in such a manner to meet the client’s analytical requirements. Additionally, data engineering is responsible for establishing practices and processes witin the aforementioned analytical architecture to ensure that meet the client’s requirements in terms of data governance, quality, lineage tracking, auditing, access control, privacy and security. As Data Engineers mature to higher levels of seniority, they increasingly take on responsibilites of mentorship towards the more junior engineers within their chapter as well.

Progression

I → II → Senior → Principal → Lead → Data Architect

Roles

The roles in Data Engineering are specified per technological stack the engineer is proficient with. This applies till the Data Architect position whereby a data architect would be required to be familiar with more than one stack.

Data Science

Data Scientists use mathematics, statistics and computer science to extract knowledge and insights from structured, semi-structured and unstructured data. Additionally, Data Scientists may be tasked with conducting research to develop new algorithms and methods to address specific challenges a customer might be facing for which no known algorithm or method could suffice as a solution. As with Data Engineers, Data Scientists increasingly take on responsibilities of mentorship towards the more junior scientists as they mature to higher levels of seniority.

Data Analysis

Data Analysts are responsible for building visualizations, dashboards, reports and other artifacts to enable discovery, insights and monitoring of information maintained in an organization’s data infrastructure. They work closely with decision makers and facilitate decision making by presenting information in manner that aids so. Data Analysts specialize in tools that are on the consumption side of an analytical architecture (such as PowerBI, Alteryx, Tableau, etc.) and it is not uncommon that analysts would have a general understanding of common business performance metrics. At the moment, the responsibilities of Data Analysts are performed by Data Engineers within Integrant. This could possibly change in the future, and in that event, this document will be updated with the associated roles, progression and scorecards.

DataOps Engineering

DataOps are responsible for setting up environments for development and testing in order to enable the rapid development of data workflows, promoting them to production and uplifting data quality standards via automation. DataOps Engineers help promote data quality by facilitating integrity tests that are run both during development and post production. They also facilitate monitoring deployed data workflows to ensure they perform as expected, and help intervene if necessary. At the moment, this role does not exist within Integrant, but could be introduced in the near future. In that event, this document will be updated with the associated roles, progression and scorecards.

MLOps Engineering

MLOps are responsible for setting up environments that enable Data Scientists the rapid prototyping of machine learning models, deploying them to production, monitoring their performance post deployment and automating the cycle to retrain and deploy newer models upon detecting performance degradation. As with DataOps, this role does not currently exist within Integrant, but could be introduced in the near future. In that event, this document will be updated with the associated roles, progression and scorecards.
